Wood Plastic Composite

Sustainable, high-performance building material combining the best of wood and polymer technology — ideal for indoor and outdoor applications.

What is WPC?

Wood Plastic Composite (WPC) is a hybrid material manufactured by blending recycled wood fibre (sawdust, wood flour, agricultural residues) with thermoplastics such as polyethylene (PE), polypropylene (PP), or polyvinyl chloride (PVC). The mixture is extruded or compression-moulded into solid profiles that combine the natural aesthetics of wood with the durability and low maintenance of plastic.

WPC is one of the fastest-growing construction materials globally, with the market projected to reach USD 10.85 billion by 2033. Its popularity is driven by rising environmental awareness — WPC utilises up to 80% recycled content, diverts plastic and wood waste from landfills, and requires no painting, staining, or sealing throughout its 20–30 year service life.

Why Choose WPC Over Natural Wood?

WPC eliminates the most common problems associated with traditional timber while delivering equal or better aesthetics.

Zero Water Absorption

Unlike natural wood, WPC does not swell, warp, cup, or rot when exposed to moisture. The plastic matrix encapsulates every wood particle, creating a waterproof barrier.

Termite & Fungus Proof

The polymer content makes WPC completely inedible for termites, borers, and fungi. No chemical treatment required — safe for food-handling areas and children's spaces.

Zero Maintenance

No painting, staining, sealing, or varnishing ever required. A simple water wash restores the original appearance. Saves 60–80% in lifecycle maintenance costs vs. wood.

100% Recyclable

WPC is fully recyclable at end of life. Manufacturing uses 60–80% recycled content. Producing WPC consumes 70% less energy than processing virgin timber.

Consistent Quality

Extrusion-controlled profiles have uniform density, colour, and dimensions — no knots, cracks, splits, or grain defects. Consistent quality across every batch.

Fire Retardant Options

WPC with fire-retardant additives achieves Class B fire rating (ASTM E84). Slower flame spread and lower smoke generation compared to natural wood.
